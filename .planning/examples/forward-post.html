I made one of these frog hotels in a shady corner of the garden, and it’s one of those small projects that actually makes sense once you see how frogs use it Tree frogs like cool, damp hiding spots during the day. A pot filled with upright PVC pipes gives them protected little spaces to rest, and if you keep the base slightly moist with rocks and a bit of water, it becomes even more inviting. The nice part is that frogs are helpful to have around the garden too. They hang around where there’s shelter, moisture, and insects. What you need: a medium or large flower pot a few PVC pipe pieces in different heights some elbow/corner pieces if you want small rocks or pebbles a little water optional: a small plant or moss around the base Steps: Put your PVC pieces upright inside the pot. Mix the heights so it looks natural and gives frogs different hiding spots. Add rocks or pebbles around the pipes to hold everything in place. Put a little water in the bottom. You don’t want it deep — just enough to keep the space humid. Add a small plant, moss, or a few damp stones around the base if you want extra cover. Place the pot in a shady, quiet spot near plants. Morning sun is fine, but full hot afternoon sun usually dries it out too fast. A couple things I’ve noticed: it works better when the area stays moist frogs seem to prefer quiet spots over busy walkways the mix of tall and short pipes gives them more places to tuck in rinsing the pot and pipes first is a good idea if they’ve been sitting in storage It’s a simple build, but it adds a little wildlife corner to the garden and helps make the space feel more alive Would you try one of these in your garden?
